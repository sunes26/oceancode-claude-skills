# Anatomy of high-scoring Show HN posts

Five posts from `patterns/corpus.json` dissected. Use as reference, not template.

---

## 1. `Show HN: This up votes itself` (3531 pts)

**Formula:** Hook (Formula 4 variant — self-referential trick)

**Why it worked:**
- Title is also the demo. Click = action.
- 5 words. Lowest character count in top 10.
- Pure curiosity, zero claim.

**Replicable for typical projects?** No. This is a category of one. Listed to show that title length can go far below median.

---

## 2. `Show HN: I made an open-source laptop from scratch` (3237 pts)

**Formula:** 1 (first-person builder) + 5 (open-source signal)

**Why it worked:**
- Concrete artifact: a physical laptop
- "from scratch" is a verifiable engineering claim
- Solo voice ("I made") signals authenticity
- Open-source = HN's value
- 9 words, 47 chars — within median

**Lesson:** Stack two formulas when both are honest signals.

---

## 3. `Show HN: If YouTube had actual channels` (2741 pts)

**Formula:** 4 (counterfactual hook)

**Why it worked:**
- Reframes a known service the reader already has an opinion on
- No product name in title — pure idea
- 6 words

**Risk:** Body has to deliver on the reframing or the title reads as bait. This one did.

**Lesson:** Reserve Formula 4 for projects that genuinely reimagine a known incumbent.

---

## 4. `Show HN: A retro video game console I've been working on in my free time` (2690 pts)

**Formula:** 3 (A + adjective) + 7 (My project framing) inside the body of the title

**Why it worked:**
- "retro" + "console" creates a clear category in 4 words
- "in my free time" is a humility signal — the opposite of metrics flex
- 14 words — over target, but the humility cadence justifies the length

**Lesson:** Length cap can be exceeded when the extra words add humility, not features.

---

## 5. `Show HN: Gemini Pro 3 imagines the HN front page 10 years from now` (3346 pts)

**Formula:** 9 (existing thing for new domain) + Formula 4 hint

**Why it worked:**
- LLM + HN-specific subject = HN crowd self-interest
- "imagines" leaves the reader curious about the output
- No claim to value, just a demo

**Risk:** Highly specific to HN audience. Same construction outside HN-aware contexts would flop.

---

# Patterns across the top 5

1. None mention founders, funding, team size, or revenue
2. None use the word "platform", "solution", or "powered by"
3. Three of five use first-person ("I", "my")
4. All five are 14 words or fewer
5. All five have a clear noun: laptop, console, channels, front page, voting trick
6. None promise an outcome — they show an artifact

This is the bar.
